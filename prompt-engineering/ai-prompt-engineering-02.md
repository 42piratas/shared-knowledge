# 1. Be Clear and Concise

The Rule: Deliver your message directly without unnecessary filler words or rambling.
Good Case: Briefly stating your main objective at the beginning of a meeting [00:15].
Bad Case: Spending several minutes on a preamble that obscures the actual point of the conversation [00:45].

# 2. Maintain Professional Attire

The Rule: Dress appropriately for the environment to convey respect and competence.
Good Case: Wearing a clean, well-fitted shirt or suit in a corporate setting [01:20].
Bad Case: Arriving at a formal client presentation in wrinkled or overly casual lounge wear [01:50].

# 3. Active Listening

The Rule: Focus entirely on the speaker and provide feedback to show understanding.
Good Case: Nodding and asking a relevant follow-up question that references what was just said [02:35].
Bad Case: Checking your phone or looking around the room while someone is explaining a task to you [03:10].
#4. Punctuality
The Rule: Value other people's time by arriving or starting on schedule.
Good Case: Logging into a virtual call two minutes early to ensure your connection works [04:05].
Bad Case: Joining a team meeting ten minutes late and asking everyone to repeat what was already covered [04:40].

# 5. Constructive Feedback

The Rule: When correcting others, focus on the work and offer a path for improvement.
Good Case: "The data in this report is great, but let's adjust the formatting to make it more readable" [05:25].
Bad Case: Telling a colleague their work is "just bad" without explaining why or how to fix it [06:00].
